Section 425(a) of the Congressional Budget Act of 1974 (2 U.S.C. 658d(a)) is amended by (A) striking and after the semicolon at the end of paragraph (1) and redesignating paragraph (2) as paragraph (3); and (B) inserting after paragraph (1) the following new paragraph: (2) any bill, joint resolution, amendment, motion, or conference report that would increase the direct costs of Federal private sector mandates (excluding any direct costs that are attributable to revenue resulting from tax or tariff provisions of any such measure if it does not raise net tax and tariff revenues over the 5fiscalyear period beginning with the first fiscal year such measure affects such revenues) by an amount that causes the thresholds specified in section 424(b)(1) to be exceeded; and. (2) The Congress has often acted without adequate information concerning the costs of private sector mandates, instead focusing only on the benefits. (3) The costs of private sector mandates are often borne in part by consumers, in the form of higher prices and reduced availability of goods and services. (4) The costs of private sector mandates are often borne in part by workers, in the form of lower wages, reduced benefits, and fewer job opportunities. (5) The costs of private sector mandates are often borne in part by small businesses, in the form of hiring disincentives and stunted growth. (B) Section 426(b)(2) of the Congressional Budget Act of 1974 (2 U.S.C. 658e(b)(2)) is amended by striking section 425 or subsection (a) of this section and inserting part B.
